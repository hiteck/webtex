Science should look better on screens than on paper.

Webtex.

Sample: Journal HTML PDF Webtex

Reading scientific articles on computers is painful when it should be a pleasure. Journals’ “electronic” articles are often atrocious, and PDF versions are usually in tiny fonts in multiple columns — because they’re meant to be printed on little pieces of paper! Computer screens are infinitely tall and there are no trees to save. Good Web typography looks very different than a typical printed page — like this.

Online scientific articles are harder to make than some things because they contain math. But they’re not that hard. It’s just that the journals don’t really care.

Webtex cares.

Leveraging 35 years of LaTeX.

LaTeX is the lingua franca of scholarly communication for physical scientists. It’s old and it’s weird, but it’s also the only way to typeset equations that look good, and there are nearly a million articles written in LaTeX on arxiv.org. A LaTeX file also contains all the information you need to create a beautiful electronic article.
